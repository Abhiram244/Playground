## 1. Setting Overview
- **Genre:** High fantasy with decent technology.
- **Current tech level:** roughly **early 2000s**.
- **State of the world:** civilizations are developed, order prevails, magic is real and in everyday use — but mystery still lingers (especially around the ancient ruins the present builds upon).
- **Tone/genre:** **Progression Fantasy** (details later) — expect a clear, climbable power scale where growth in psychic/arcane might is central.

## 2. Cosmology — The Three Dimensions
The cosmos is built from three primary dimensions:

1. **The Living World** — the world of the living.
2. **The Veil** — the barrier between the living and the dead, and a full dimension in its own right.
3. **The Afterlife (World of the Dead)** — where souls go once truly, irreversibly dead.

### Magic across the dimensions
| Dimension | Magic Strength | Magic Restriction |
|---|---|---|
| Afterlife (Dead) | Strongest | Most restricted |
| The Veil | 2nd strongest | Unrestricted |
| Living World | Weakest (3rd) | Unrestricted |

- **Net result:** The **Veil is practically the best dimension to wield magic** — very strong *and* unrestricted. (Living World is also unrestricted, just weaker.)
- The source material barely explores the **World of the Dead**, so it is intentionally left **vague**.

## 3. The Veil (mechanics)
**Purpose:** A dimension that is also the membrane between living and dead.

### Entry conditions
To enter the Veil, a host must be **"dead enough"** that their soul no longer belongs to the living world, **but not so dead** that the soul is sent to the afterlife. That liminal state lands the soul in the Veil.
- The **body must be recoverable** (soul detaches from the living, but the body is not irreversibly destroyed).
- **Entering the Veil is very hard.**

### Exits from the Veil
- **Body healed / recovered** → soul returns to the **Living World**.
- **Body completely dies / becomes unrecoverable** → soul passes to the **Afterlife**.
  - You **cannot deliberately exit the Veil into the afterlife**, and from within the Veil you **cannot will yourself to influence the living world / your own body** to force a return.
  - **Very rare exceptions exist** to this rule.

### Gear & dimensional transition rule
- **Non-magical gear does NOT travel** with you between dimensions (into the Veil, or Veil → Living).
- **Magical artifacts DO travel** with you — they are **bound to you**.
  - Rarity of magical artifacts: **rare, but not legendary-rare.**

## 4. History of the Living World

### 4.1 The Ancient Civilization & the apparent paradox
- The present world is built upon the **ruins of an ancient civilization**.
- **Apparent paradox:** the ancient civ looks *less* advanced than the present one.
  - **Why it looks that way:** the current civ can only see the **structures the ancients built in the living world**, which they **left ~1,450 years ago**. Those are frozen at pre-Transfer tech.
  - After leaving, the ancients had **~1,000 years to keep progressing inside the Veil** — progress no one in the living world can see.
  - **The truth (player ruling):** even counting their Veil progress, the ancient civ — *at its peak* — was **NOT more advanced** than the present empire. And regardless, **today the empire is gone / collapsed.**
- **First discovery:** the current civ did **not** find any ancient structure during the 50 years of overlap. The **first ancient structure was discovered ~300 years ago** and was labeled something like a **"mass grave."** (The ancient civ = the Veil empire, sustained by **automated labyrinths** providing life-support to bodies and thus keeping souls in the Veil; the current civ = living world. The two had no way to know of or communicate with each other.)

### 4.2 The Great Transfer (≈1,450 years ago) — *unknown to all living people so far*
- The ancients discovered the **Veil is far more suitable than the living world** (strong, unrestricted magic).
- They built **massive techno-arcane facilities** to systematically **suspend people between life and death**, transferring the empire's population *en masse* into the Veil.
- In the Veil they founded a **thriving mega-empire** (see §4c).
- The life-support mechanisms were advanced enough to **withstand any single natural disaster**, and did so **for a millennium**.

### 4.3 The Fall (≈450 years ago) — overnight collapse
- The systems could survive any one disaster, **but not multiple high-magnitude disasters at once**.
- By ill fortune (arguably destined — they'd already beaten millennium-long odds), the system was struck **simultaneously** by a **Category-5 tornado** and a **magnitude-8 earthquake**.
- Life support broke → **the empire's citizens were damned to the afterlife** → the mega-empire **collapsed overnight**.

### 4.4 The Emperor & the survivors (the vigil)
- **The Emperor** and a **few others** survived the initial collapse — purely through their **unimaginable arcane pools** and **unimaginably strong Veil-forged arcane relics**.
- Their **living-world bodies were already rotten**, so they persisted in the Veil only by their **psyche fighting the essence of the Veil / the order of the universe itself**.
- The **Emperor specifically resisted the order of the universe for 50–60 years** by pure might before finally losing.
- The **other survivors completely burned out** — fully gone, nothing meaningful left. **The Emperor is the only one who still matters in any way.**
- When they perished, their **souls were NOT sent to the afterlife** — they were **(mostly) eroded away** by the act of fighting the universal order.
- Their **relics were left clinging to their dead "bodies"** (in essence, within the Veil — not literal corpses in the living world).

## 4c. The Veil Mega-Empire
- Founded by the ancients after the Great Transfer; thrived a millennium before the Fall.
- **Player ruling: further detail is NOT needed for the campaign.** What matters is its ruins, defenses, the palace, and the Emperor (all covered).

## 4b. The Emperor (current state) & the Imperial Palace

### Psychic-might scale (canonical reference)
Using a single normal living-world mage as the baseline unit:
| Subject | Psychic might |
|---|---|
| Normal mage — in Living World | a **bucket** |
| Normal mage — in the Veil | a **drum** |
| Normal mage — in the Underworld/Afterlife | **5 drums** |
| Strongest mages of the **CURRENT** age (in Living World) | a **water tower** |
| The **live Emperor at his peak** (in the Veil) | an **ocean** |
| The Emperor **now** (corpse, no master) | the **Caspian Sea** — tiny vs. an ocean, but still *infinitely* larger than a water tower |

### The Emperor's corpse
- He is now a **corpse seated on a throne of gold** in the **throne room of the Imperial Palace** — the former capital of the Veil empire.
- He wears the **5 most powerful relics**. They *could* be taken by others (he is no longer present to defend them), but doing so is **no easy task and has never been done**.
- His residual psychic might (~Caspian Sea) has **no master to direct it**; it just **clings to the corpse and leaks psychic essence**.

### The Beacon (leaking essence)
- The leak acts as a **beacon visible across the entire Veil**.
- **Falloff:** concentration **drops by a factor of 8 each time distance doubles** (and rises ×8 each time distance halves).
- **In the surrounding areas of the palace:** the undirected essence is **beneficial** — free power you can absorb to empower yourself.
- **In the throne room and beyond:** it is a **drowning force**.

### Essence escalation inside the Imperial Palace
- As the PC moves **deeper into the palace**, essence concentration **steadily increases**.
- **Within the throne room:** like a **flood — but manageable**.
- **Close to the Emperor's body:** a **FLOOD of psychic essence that drowns most users**.
- **Deflector relics:** along the way the PC can find relics that act as **deflectors**, **extending how long they can withstand proximity** — but **none can make it safe**.
- **Implication:** if the PC chooses to climb the **100 steps** to the throne, they **must be quick.**

### Throne room layout & the relics
- At the **base** of the throne room sit **3 very powerful relics**, each **encased in glass** (not worn by the Emperor).
- From that base, you must **climb 100 steps** to reach the throne (and the corpse + its 5 relics).

**Relic mobility & binding rules:**
- The **3 base relics** are **non-equipable and non-movable** — you can *use* them in place, but you cannot take them with you.
- The **5 relics on the Emperor's corpse** are **equipable** and **can be bound to a new user** (taking them is extremely hard and has never been done).

**Named relic — "The Sundering Needle"** *(confirmed name):*
- One of the **3 base relics**. Like the others, it is **one-of-a-kind** and **extremely powerful**, and it is **non-movable**.
- Power: it can **temporarily cut a small, precise hole in the barrier between dimensions**, letting a user pass between them.
- Routes: **Veil → Living World**, or **Veil → Underworld**.
- **Significance:** this is the relic a user (e.g. the MC) can use to **return to the living world.**

## 5b. Player Progression Tracking (meta)
- **For the player (MC):** progression is tracked as a **percentage** (plot/positional progress toward the throne + escape, not raw power). I'll report a **% estimate** at milestones.

### Power system (how the MC grows)
Two complementary growth vectors:
1. **Acquiring relics** → **instant power spikes** (discrete, sudden jumps when found/equipped/bound).
2. **Absorbing the Emperor's background essence** → **constant, gradual growth** (passive, ambient — the undirected beacon essence; stronger the closer you are, within survivable limits).

*Summary: relics = burst power; background essence = steady passive scaling.*

### Progression map (canonical)
| % | Danger | What's happening |
|---|---|---|
| **0%** | — | The start. |
| **1–5%** | No danger | Wounded & on life support; can still **talk and observe**, but the world feels **increasingly distant** over time. |
| **5–10%** | Low | **Partially entered the Veil**; can **sometimes feel their body**, but the sensation is fading. |
| **10–50%** | Low→Medium | Killing **golems & spirits**, **finding & equipping relics**, growing more powerful. |
| **50%** | Peaceful | Finds a **peaceful settlement of souls** who **verbally & repeatedly warn** the PC **not to go further** — it's dangerous. |
| **50–60%** | Very low | Exploring **ruins & structures**; awfully **few creatures**. |
| **60–65%** | Medium | Equipping artifacts, killing golems/spirits. |
| **65–80%** | Medium | Routine (artifacts, combat). |
| **80–90%** | Peaceful | Basically **no enemies — just emptiness**. |
| **90–95%** | **HARD+** | Remnants of the **imperial palace defenses**: countless golems, spirits, traps. **Most defenses have failed with time, so it's beatable.** |
| **95–100%** | Peaceful\* | **No defenses inside the palace itself**, but it's a **maze of countless rooms**; plus the **throne room** (see essence-flood rules). |
| **100%** | — | **Return to the world of the living.** |

## 7. Starting Plot (the MC's beginning)
- The **MC** is a **prodigy** from a **medium-sized town**.
- A **dragon escaped** because a **careless merchant cut corners on safety**, causing a disaster in the town.
- During the disaster, the MC **tries to save his love interest** and is **heavily burned** in the process.
- The **dragon is killed**, but the MC is left with a **mortal wound** and is placed on **life support**.
- The wounds are **too deep to heal**, but he can be **kept barely alive** — i.e. his body is **"dead enough" but still recoverable**: the exact liminal state that sends a soul to the **Veil**.
- The MC's **soul is sent to the Veil**. He finds himself on a **vast flatland dotted with massive structures**, populated by **spirits/golems he must defeat**.
